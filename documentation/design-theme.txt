                         TYPOGRAPHY: PERSONALITY OVER POLISH
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PRIMARY: Inter
  Clean. Functional. But we use it BOLDLY.
  Weights: 400 (body), 600 (emphasis), 700 (headers that mean business)

DISPLAY: Bricolage Grotesque
  Quirky without being precious. Marketing moments. Feature announcements.
  Use sparingly—this is the spice, not the meal.

SIZE STRATEGY (Anti-minimalist):
  Dashboard numbers: HUGE (3xl-6xl). Make revenue feel important.
  Body text: Comfortable (0.8rem base). Not trying to be "clean" at expense of readability.
  Labels: Small but BOLD (0.6rem, weight 700). Shouting in a whisper.

UNUSUAL CHOICES:
  - All-caps labels (11px, 700 weight, 0.1em tracking) for military precision
  - Tabular numbers ALWAYS for financial data (font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums)
  - Generous line-height (1.6-1.8) because cramped text is for spreadsheets
  - Drop the obsession with "elegant" small sizes—go BIGGER on key metrics

                          SPACING: ASYMMETRIC TENSION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Forget the "8px grid religion." Use rhythm that feels RIGHT:

CARD PADDING:
  - Standard card: 24px (not 20, not 16. Own it.)
  - Stat cards: 28px top/bottom, 32px left/right (give those numbers room to breathe)
  - Dense tables: 16px cells (pack data, not fluff)

VERTICAL RHYTHM:
  - Section gaps: 64px (double the standard SaaS 32px)
  - Card stacks: 20px (prime number, feels less mechanical)
  - List items: 14px (just off from 16px—creates subtle tension)

HORIZONTAL:
  - Dashboard: 3-column on desktop but use 1.2fr 1fr 0.8fr ratios (not equal)
  - Sidebar: 280px (not 256, not 300—specific, intentional)
  - Max content width: 1400px (wide enough to show data density)

COLOR STRATEGY (Not rainbow—purposeful):
  Primary metric:     #ce6203 (forge orange)
  Secondary metric:   #445642 (ledger green)
  Tertiary metric:    #fffad6 (parchment)
  Positive trend:     #84cc16 (electric lime)
  Negative trend:     #dc2626 (crimson)
  Neutral/Comparison: #6b7280 (smoke)

BAR CHART:
  - Bar width: variable based on data range, but minimum 24px
  - Rounded top corners: 6px
  - Flat bottom (no rounding—grounded)
  - Gradient fill: subtle top-to-bottom (lighter at top)
  - Hover: bar lifts with shadow, tooltip appears
  - Spacing between bars: 12px (generous—let bars breathe)
  - Grid lines: 1px dashed #374151, 40% opacity

LINE CHART:
  - Line thickness: 3px (substantial, not hairline)
  - Points: 8px circles, white border (2px), colored fill
  - Hover point: scales to 12px
  - Area under line: gradient from color (30% opacity) to transparent
  - Multiple lines: use varying stroke styles (solid, dashed, dotted) not just color
  - Crosshair on hover: thin lines extending to axes

PIE/DONUT CHART:
  - Stroke between segments: 2px #0e1616 (clean separation)
  - Hover segment: scale 1.05, shadow appears
  - Center label (donut): Large number (36px), small descriptor below (11px)
  - Legend: positioned to right, dot + label, click to toggle segment
  - Avoid rainbow—use 3-4 colors max, varying shades

SPARKLINES (Inline mini-charts):
  - Height: 40px, Width: 80px
  - No axes, no labels—pure trend
  - Line: 2px, single color
  - Last point highlighted: 6px circle
  - Use in stat cards for quick trend indication

  bg: #0e1616 (dark, not card color)
  border: 1.5px solid #ce6203 (accent border)
  padding: 12px 16px

                           THE QUOTLA AESTHETIC
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

NOT: Clean, minimal, modern SaaS
YES: Dark, confident, business-focused with moments of unexpected warmth

NOT: Purple gradients, bubbly cards, playful illustrations
YES: Amber fire, architectural lines, data density with style

NOT: Perfectly symmetrical 8px grids
YES: Intentional asymmetry, prime number spacing, visual rhythm

NOT: Passive, friendly, approachable
YES: Commanding, professional, trustworthy with character

INSPIRATION:
- Art Deco ledgers and accounting books
- Brutalist architecture (function-first beauty)
- High-contrast film noir lighting
- Forge fire and leather-bound tomes
- Terminal interfaces (information density)

THE RESULT:
A business tool that feels SERIOUS without being STERILE.
Professional without being BORING.
Functional without being GENERIC.
